About Me

I am Cameron Hawkins, a passionate elementary educator committed to enhancing student learning through innovative and engaging methods. My career in education began after obtaining a Bachelor of Science in Education from the University of Georgia. I continued to expand my expertise by pursuing a Master of Arts in Teaching from Liberty University while actively teaching. Over the years, I've taught at various elementary levels, from Kindergarten through 2nd grade, and more recently, I have extended my reach to include virtual tutoring for 1st to 5th graders across four states. A significant highlight of my career was initiating Cobb County's first virtual elementary program, a pioneering effort that integrated traditional teaching methods with advanced technology to enrich the learning experience significantly.

This diverse experience has not only broadened my educational approach but also deepened my understanding of how technology can transform teaching. Outside the classroom, my personal life is filled with activities that enrich my professional endeavors, including fitness, sewing, and self-taught coding. These hobbies inspire me to continually seek innovative ways to incorporate technology and creativity into my teaching practices.

Teaching Philosophy

  • Empowerment
  • Student-Led
  • Diverse Methods
  • Supportive Community

Empowerment through Confidence and Curiosity

My ultimate goal as an educator is to empower students to feel confident in their abilities and to cultivate a lifelong love and curiosity for learning. By fostering an environment where students feel safe to explore and question, I aim to inspire a deep-seated enthusiasm for discovery and intellectual growth.

Student-Led Learning

Inspired by the explorative approach of Miss Frizzle from "The Magic School Bus," my teaching style prioritizes student-led learning. This method encourages students to take the wheel in their educational journey, making learning experiences more relevant and engaging. By allowing students to lead, they develop critical thinking and problem-solving skills that are crucial for their future success.

Diverse Methods for Diverse Learners

Recognizing that each student is unique, I employ a variety of teaching methods such as project-based learning, collaborative assignments, hands-on activities, and technology integration. These approaches ensure that learning is accessible and engaging for all students, accommodating different learning styles and fostering a comprehensive educational experience.

A Supportive and Respectful Community

My classroom is more than just a learning space; it's a family where respect and responsibility are paramount. By cultivating a supportive atmosphere, I encourage students to express themselves and respect their peers. This community-focused environment helps students feel valued and secure, which is essential for effective learning and personal growth.

Classroom Management Strategies and Policies

In my classroom, the first week is dedicated to thoroughly teaching all procedures and expectations, setting the foundational understanding crucial for a successful academic year. This period focuses on setting the tone for our classroom environment, where every routineâ€”from morning arrivals to dismissal proceduresâ€”is explicitly explained, practiced, and reinforced.

My approach is built on the belief that students thrive in structured environments. By holding students accountable and setting clear expectations, they rise to the challenge and engage more effectively in their learning journey. This structure is not just about maintaining order; it's about creating a nurturing space where students feel secure and encouraged to participate actively.

Each activity in my classroom has a specific procedure, whether it's lining up quietly, managing transitions, or engaging during lesson time. For instance, I use a variety of attention signals to focus the class and maintain engagement. These strategies help in minimizing disruptions and maximizing learning time.

By establishing a classroom that functions like a well-oiled machine, students understand their responsibilities and the classroom expectations, allowing them to focus on learning.

Technology Integration in the Classroom

  • Grading and Data Management
  • Student Assignments
  • Digital Portfolios

Grading and Data Management

I utilize a fully digital system for recording grades and managing student data, which allows for seamless tracking and analysis of student performance. This digital approach enables me to quickly access and synthesize data, aiding in the timely adjustment of instructional strategies to better meet student needs.

Student Assignments

In my practice, I incorporate virtual choice boards for independent work, which empowers students by providing them with agency over their learning paths. Additionally, I use interactive platforms like Classkick and Nearpod to facilitate real-time feedback and engagement. These tools are integral for maintaining organized and accessible digital portfolios, which showcase student work and progress throughout the school year.

Digital Portfolios

I have successfully implemented district-specific platforms that maintain comprehensive digital portfolios for each student. These portfolios serve as a dynamic record of student achievements and are easily shareable with parents and administrators, enhancing communication and transparency.
